Platform Features

What Gets Built

A full-stack marketing platform covering every channel and function the business needs — designed from day one to support multiple companies under one roof.

Lead Capture & CRM

  • Custom landing pages per service line — residential re-roof, commercial, and storm/insurance
  • Inbound lead forms with source tracking — know exactly which channel and campaign drove each lead
  • Lightweight CRM view to manage, assign, and follow up on leads without a third-party tool
  • Call tracking integration so phone leads are attributed to the right campaign

Campaign Management

  • Internal dashboard to build, launch, and monitor Google Ads and Meta campaigns directly
  • No agency approval cycles — campaigns can go live the same day a storm event hits
  • Pre-built campaign templates per service line, ready to activate with minimal setup
  • Budget controls and spend alerts to keep ad spend from running unchecked

Email & SMS Automation

  • Automated drip sequences triggered by lead source, service type, and funnel stage
  • Residential, commercial, and insurance leads each get messaging tailored to their situation
  • Post-estimate and post-job follow-up sequences to close more deals and generate reviews
  • Sequences are built once and run automatically — no manual outreach required

AI-Powered Content

  • Claude generates ad copy variations for all three service lines on demand
  • SEO blog posts, service pages, and local content written and updated without a copywriter
  • Email and SMS campaign content produced in minutes, not days
  • Consistent brand voice maintained across every piece of content, every market

Analytics & Reporting

  • Real-time dashboard showing cost-per-lead, conversion rates, and channel ROI
  • Cross-company reporting once acquisitions are live — all data visible in one place
  • Campaign performance history builds over time, making future decisions smarter
  • Monthly summary reports generated and delivered automatically to stakeholders

Storm Surge Automation

  • Logic that monitors hail and storm event data by zip code in real time
  • When a storm event is detected, pre-built campaigns auto-activate for the affected area
  • Landing pages and ad copy pre-loaded for storm/insurance work — no scrambling when it counts
  • One of the highest-leverage features for a company that works storm/insurance jobs

Reputation & Reviews

  • Automated review request sent to customers after job completion via SMS and email
  • Google Business profile management across all company locations from a single dashboard
  • Monitor and respond to new reviews without logging in and out of multiple accounts
  • Review volume is one of the strongest local SEO signals — this runs it on autopilot

Multi-Brand White Label

  • Each acquired company gets their own branded instance — logo, colors, phone numbers, service area
  • Local identity preserved while every company runs on the same platform underneath
  • New company instances are created through configuration, not custom development
  • The entire feature set above is available to every company on the platform from day one
The Build Engine

The Claude Trio

Three tools working together — each playing a distinct role in designing, building, and operating this platform. This combination is what makes an internal build achievable without a traditional agency or large dev team.

Claude Chat — The Strategist

  • Architecture planning — Work through what to build first, how features connect, and what delivers the fastest return before writing a single line of code
  • Ad copy and campaign writing — Generate Google and Meta ad variations for residential re-roofs, storm damage, and commercial bids on demand
  • Email and SMS sequences — Build full drip campaign copy tailored to each lead type and funnel stage without hiring a copywriter
  • SEO content strategy — Identify local search keywords per market, draft blog posts, and write service pages that rank in each company's area
  • Competitive research — Analyze local competitors in any new market before a campaign or acquisition goes live
  • Onboarding documentation — Create SOPs and training materials for staff at newly acquired companies joining the platform
  • Ongoing content engine — As documented by real marketing teams in 2025–2026, Claude Chat functions as a persistent content machine that maintains brand voice across every channel, every market, without additional headcount

Claude Code — The Builder

  • Full platform development — Builds the entire codebase: frontend dashboard, backend API, database, and authentication — not just snippets or prototypes
  • Ad platform integrations — Connects directly to Google Ads and Meta Ads APIs so campaigns can be launched and monitored from the internal tool, no agency access needed
  • Multi-tenant architecture — Designs and implements the database and routing logic so each acquired company's data is isolated but visible centrally
  • Landing page generator — Builds a templating system that produces fully branded landing pages for a new company in hours, not weeks
  • Automated reporting pipelines — Pulls data from ad platforms nightly, compiles cost-per-lead and ROI reports, and pushes them to the dashboard automatically
  • Storm surge logic — Monitors weather and hail event data by zip code and triggers pre-built campaigns when conditions match, with no manual input
  • Real-world precedent — Teams documented in early 2026 use Claude Code to ship landing pages, CRM-connected workflows, and live ad pipelines without traditional engineering bottlenecks — the tool bridges the gap between marketing strategy and working software

Cowork — The Operator

  • Campaign scheduling workflows — Marketing staff trigger pre-built workflows to launch seasonal or storm campaigns without touching code or waiting on a developer
  • Monthly report delivery — Auto-compiles performance summaries per company and sends them to the right people on schedule, every month
  • Lead routing — When a new lead comes in, Cowork routes it to the right rep based on service type and geography, automatically
  • Review request triggers — Post-job completion, Cowork fires the review request sequence — no manual follow-up, no leads that fall through the cracks
  • Acquisition onboarding flow — When a new company joins the platform, a Cowork workflow provisions their instance, populates brand assets, and notifies the team
  • Budget and performance alerts — Sends automated notifications when a campaign exceeds spend limits or falls below performance benchmarks
  • The key distinction — Where Claude Code builds the platform, Cowork operates it day-to-day, putting automation in the hands of non-technical staff so the system runs without constant engineering involvement

The Bigger Picture

Plug & Play Acquisitions

The platform isn't just a cost-cutting tool for one company — it's infrastructure that every future acquisition connects to from day one, without a custom build or new agency relationship each time.

How Onboarding an Acquisition Works

  • Deal closes — A Cowork workflow is triggered automatically, creating a new tenant, assigning a subdomain, and alerting the setup team
  • Brand assets loaded — Logo, colors, service area, and local phone numbers are entered. Landing pages and ad templates populate from there
  • Campaigns go live — Pre-built campaign templates for residential, commercial, and storm work are activated for the new service area immediately
  • Team gets access — Local staff log in with role-based permissions — they see only their company's data, while headquarters sees everything across all companies
  • Reporting rolls up — The new company's leads, spend, and performance appear on the central dashboard alongside every other company in the portfolio

What This Means at Scale

  • No per-company agency retainers — Each acquisition uses the same platform. No new contracts, no onboarding fees, no months of ramp-up time before marketing is running
  • Centralized intelligence — As more companies join, the platform accumulates data on what campaigns, copy, and channels convert best for roofing across different markets — each new company benefits from what the last one learned
  • Consistent brand, local feel — Each company keeps its local identity and reputation while benefiting from enterprise-grade marketing infrastructure behind the scenes
  • HQ visibility without micromanagement — Leadership sees cross-company performance in real time without needing to log into each company's tools separately
  • The platform becomes a competitive asset — As the acquisition portfolio grows, the marketing infrastructure is itself a differentiator — a capability that independent roofing companies can't replicate on their own, and one that makes each acquisition more valuable faster
